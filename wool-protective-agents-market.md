<p><p>Ionic and nonionic wool-protective agents are both used to prevent damage to wool fibers during processing. Ionic agents work by forming strong electrostatic bonds with the wool fibers, providing protection against harsh chemicals and mechanical stress. Nonionic agents, on the other hand, form weaker bonds with the fibers, providing a more gentle form of protection. Both types of agents are essential for maintaining the integrity and quality of wool products in various industries.</p></p>
