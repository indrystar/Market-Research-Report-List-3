<p><p>Pertussis vaccine market includes two main types of vaccines: whole-cell vaccines and acellular vaccines. Whole-cell vaccines contain killed Bordetella pertussis bacteria and are known for their high effectiveness but are associated with more side effects. Acellular vaccines, on the other hand, contain purified components of the bacteria, resulting in fewer side effects but also slightly lower effectiveness. Both types of vaccines are used globally to protect individuals, particularly infants and young children, against pertussis, also known as whooping cough.</p></p>
