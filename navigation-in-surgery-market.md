<p><p>Navigation in surgery involves the use of technology to guide surgeons during procedures. Optical Surgical Navigation Systems use cameras and tracking markers to create real-time 3D models of the patient's anatomy. Electromagnetic Surgical Navigation Systems use electromagnetic fields to track surgical instruments and their position in relation to the patient's body. Other emerging navigation systems include robotic navigation systems and augmented reality systems, which overlay virtual images onto the patient's anatomy to assist surgeons during complex procedures. These technologies improve accuracy, reduce risk, and enhance surgical outcomes.</p></p>
