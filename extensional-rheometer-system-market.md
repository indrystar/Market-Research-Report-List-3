<p><p>Extensional rheometers are used to measure the extensional properties of materials, such as viscosity, elasticity, and flow behavior. The market for extensional rheometer systems can be categorized into three types: dynamic type, capillary type, and others. Dynamic type rheometers apply an oscillating force to measure viscoelastic properties. Capillary type rheometers measure elongational viscosity by forcing the sample through a capillary. Other types may include specialized instruments for specific applications or industries. Each type offers unique capabilities for studying material behavior under extensional conditions.</p></p>
