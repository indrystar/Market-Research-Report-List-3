<p><p>Organic Light Emitting Diode (OLED) Displays come in two main types: Active-Matrix OLED (AMOLED) and Passive Matrix OLED (PMOLED). </p><p>AMOLED displays use a thin-film transistor backplane to control each individual pixel, resulting in higher resolution and faster response times. They are commonly found in high-end smartphones and TVs.</p><p>PMOLED displays, on the other hand, are simpler and less expensive, making them ideal for small screens like smartwatches and MP3 players.</p><p>Both types offer vibrant colors, high contrast ratios, and excellent visibility in various lighting conditions.</p></p>
