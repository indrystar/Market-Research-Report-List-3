<p><p>Clinical trinocular microscopes come in two main types: inverted type and upright type. Inverted type microscopes are designed with the objective lens placed underneath the stage, allowing for observation of samples in containers or flasks. Upright type microscopes have the objective lens positioned above the stage, making them suitable for traditional slide-mounted specimens. Both types are commonly used in clinical settings for analyzing biological samples and conducting research. Each type offers unique features and advantages depending on the specific application.</p></p>
