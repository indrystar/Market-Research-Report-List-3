<p><p>Injectable anticoagulants are medications that help prevent blood clots in patients at risk for thrombosis. The market for these drugs includes Low Molecular Weight Heparin (LMWH), Unfractionated Heparin, Fibrinolytics, and other types of anticoagulants. LMWH is a commonly used anticoagulant that has a longer duration of action and can be administered at home. Unfractionated Heparin is a traditional anticoagulant often used in hospital settings. Fibrinolytics are enzymes that help dissolve blood clots. Other types of anticoagulants include novel oral anticoagulants and parenteral direct thrombin inhibitors.</p></p>
