<p><ul><li>Root-form Dental Implants</li><li>Plate-form Dental Implants</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Root-form dental implants are a type of tapered implant that closely resembles the natural root of a tooth. They are commonly used for patients with adequate bone density and width. Plate-form dental implants, on the other hand, are wider and flatter, making them suitable for patients with narrower jawbones or those who have experienced bone loss. Both types of tapered implants offer stable support for dental prosthetics and can provide long-lasting, natural-looking results for patients in need of tooth replacement.</p></p>
