<p><p>The rabies vaccine market is divided into two main applications: pre-exposure prophylaxis and post-exposure prophylaxis. Pre-exposure prophylaxis involves administering the vaccine to individuals at high risk of exposure to rabies, such as veterinarians and travelers to rabies-endemic areas. Post-exposure prophylaxis, on the other hand, involves administering the vaccine to individuals after they have been exposed to the rabies virus, in order to prevent the onset of the disease. Both applications play a crucial role in preventing the spread of rabies.</p></p>
